About Me

I am a linguist working on Romance languages, investigating how commitment shapes common ground at the interface of formal semantics, pragmatics, and syntax, with particular attention to morphological meaning such as verbal mood.

I earned my PhD in linguistics from the Universtiat Pompeu Fabra Barcelona in 2025, with a thesis on the interrelation between mood (alternation) and commitments. I am currently affiliated with the Universitat Pompeu Fabra as a visiting researcher.

Beyond mood and common ground, my research encompasses a wide range of topics — experimental methods, verum, exclamatives, non-canonical speech acts, and obviation — all often examined in the context of cross-linguistics.

More information about my research can be found in the publications section, where I describe the questions I study and how I approach them. If you’d like to get in touch — whether with questions, ideas, or collaboration proposals — I’d be happy to hear from you.
